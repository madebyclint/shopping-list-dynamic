## Deeper View

**Yucatecan Tamales + Esquites** *(Monday, June 8)*
Total time: ~45–60 min · Approx calories: ~620 per adult

> Yucatecan tamales are wrapped in softened banana leaves rather than dried corn husks — they steam to a firmer, slightly smoky masa and unwrap like a little gift at the table. Achiote paste (recado rojo) stains the masa a deep brick-orange and gives the filling a distinctly earthy, citrus-spiked flavor that's unlike anything a regular tamal tastes like. Esquites — warm street corn in a cup — brings the cool mayo-lime-cotija contrast that makes every bite of the tamal taste brighter. Mango slices close the meal.

**Ingredients Used**

- Banana leaves (frozen pack, softened over flame or hot water)
- Masa harina (2 cups dry)
- Achiote paste or annatto powder (2–3 tbsp)
- Chicken broth (2–2½ cups, warm)
- Lard or vegetable shortening (½ cup)
- Rotisserie chicken (1 bird, pulled — seasoned with achiote, cumin, oregano, orange juice)
- Jalapeño or habanero (optional, chopped fine)
- Corn, 3–4 ears (or 2 cups frozen kernels)
- Mayo (2 tbsp), lime, chili powder, cotija cheese (¼ cup crumbled)
- Mango (2, sliced) — served on the side

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pass banana leaf sections over an open flame or submerge in very hot water 2–3 min until pliable and bright green. Wipe dry. Tear into ~10×12" rectangles.
2. Beat lard or shortening until fluffy, about 2 min. Add masa harina gradually, alternating with warm chicken broth, mixing until dough is a soft peanut butter consistency. Beat in achiote paste and 1 tsp salt. Dough should be slightly tacky and hold its shape.
3. Season pulled rotisserie chicken with a pinch of achiote, cumin, dried oregano, and a squeeze of orange juice. Add jalapeño or habanero if using. Taste and adjust salt.
4. Spread 3–4 tbsp of masa in the center of each leaf rectangle (leaving 2" borders). Add 2 tbsp chicken filling. Fold sides over the filling, then fold top and bottom in. Tie closed with a torn strip of banana leaf or kitchen twine.
5. Stand tamales upright in a steamer basket. Steam over medium heat, covered, 45 min. Let rest 5 min before unwrapping — they firm up as they cool slightly.
6. While tamales steam: heat a skillet over medium-high. Add corn kernels (fresh cut from cob or thawed frozen), char slightly 3–4 min. Off heat, stir in 2 tbsp mayo, a squeeze of lime, ½ tsp chili powder, and crumbled cotija. Season to taste.
7. Serve tamales unwrapped at the table with esquites alongside. Finish with mango slices.

**Chilaquiles Divorciados** *(Tuesday, June 9 — FAST)*
Total time: ~15–20 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

> Chilaquiles divorciados — divorced chilaquiles — splits a plate in half: salsa roja on one side, salsa verde on the other. The chips never see each other's sauce, just like a couple that can't agree on anything. The leftover rotisserie chicken from Monday gets pulled cold and laid on top, the fried egg anchors the whole plate, and crema, cotija, and avocado do the finishing work. It's a 20-minute meal that looks like effort.

**Ingredients Used**

- Tortilla chips (about 4–5 cups / half a bag)
- Salsa verde (1 jar)
- Salsa roja (1 jar)
- Rotisserie chicken (second bird from Monday, pulled and refrigerated)
- Eggs (4, fried — optional but excellent)
- Crema or sour cream (for drizzling)
- Cotija or queso fresco (crumbled, for topping)
- Avocados (2, sliced or smashed)
- Cilantro (fresh, for garnish)
- Lime (wedges)
- Oranges (sliced, served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Heat salsa verde in one skillet, salsa roja in a second skillet simultaneously over medium heat. Bring each to a gentle simmer.
2. Divide tortilla chips roughly in half. Add one half to the verde pan, the other half to the roja pan. Toss quickly to coat — chips should soften but not turn mushy (30 seconds max).
3. Plate each portion side by side on the same plate — roja on one half, verde on the other. They're divorced; keep them separated.
4. Top both sides with shredded rotisserie chicken. Fry eggs separately and lay one on top of each plate if using.
5. Finish with a drizzle of crema, crumbled cotija, sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
6. Serve with orange slices on the side.

**Battered Fried Chicken + Watermelon** *(Wednesday, June 10)*
Total time: ~40–50 min · Approx calories: ~650 per adult

> A milk batter — flour, baking powder, eggs, and Lactaid whole milk — creates a coating that fries up puffy and crackly, almost tempura-thick in spots, with a tender steam pocket between crust and meat. Dutch oven frying keeps the oil temperature stable and prevents splattering. Cut the watermelon cold and serve it straight — no prep, just the perfect counterpoint.

**Ingredients Used**

- Chicken thighs + drumsticks (3–4 lbs)
- All-purpose flour (1 cup, plus extra for dusting)
- Baking powder (1 tsp)
- Salt (1 tsp)
- Eggs (2)
- Lactaid whole milk (¾ cup)
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp, for batter)
- Garlic powder, paprika, cayenne, black pepper (for seasoning)
- Vegetable oil for frying (2–3 inches in Dutch oven, ~1 qt)
- Watermelon (half the melon — save the other half for Friday's agua fresca)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pat chicken completely dry with paper towels — moisture is the enemy of batter adhesion. Season generously with salt, garlic powder, paprika, cayenne, and black pepper. Dust lightly with flour; shake off excess.
2. Whisk dry batter: 1 cup flour, 1 tsp baking powder, 1 tsp salt, pinch of garlic powder and paprika. In a separate bowl beat 2 eggs with ¾ cup Lactaid milk and 1 tbsp oil. Combine wet into dry and stir until just smooth (a few lumps are fine).
3. Pour 2–3 inches of vegetable oil into a Dutch oven. Heat to 375°F over medium-high. Use a thermometer.
4. Dip chicken pieces in batter, letting excess drip off. Fry in batches of 2–3 pieces — don't crowd the pot. Maintain oil between 350–375°F. Fry 10–12 min, turning occasionally, until deep golden brown and cooked through (165°F internal).
5. Drain on a wire rack or paper towels. Keep finished pieces warm in a 300°F oven while frying remaining batches.
6. Slice watermelon cold and serve alongside.

**Maggi Beef Ribs + Kimchi** *(Thursday, June 11 — TEEN PREP)*
Total time: 8 min cook (+ overnight marinade) · Approx calories: ~560 per adult

> Flanken-cut short ribs — thin cross-cut strips with bone sections running through — are the secret weapon here. The marinade is nothing but Maggi seasoning sauce and black pepper, and the overnight refrigerator time does all the work. The air fryer at 450°F gives them a deep, almost char-grilled caramelization in 8 minutes flat. Perfect teen territory: no technique required, just pull from the fridge and push the button.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef short ribs, flanken cut (2–3 lbs — thin cross-cut strips, ask the butcher)
- Maggi seasoning sauce (generous pour)
- Black pepper
- Store-bought kimchi (1 jar)
- Jasmine rice (2 cups dry)
- Pineapple or seasonal fruit (for the side)

**Night Before (Parent or Teen)**

1. Pat ribs dry with paper towels.
2. Lay ribs in a dish or zip bag. Pour Maggi seasoning generously over all surfaces — you want a thick coating, not just a drizzle. Add several cracks of black pepper.
3. Rub the sauce in with your hands, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

**Teen Steps Day-Of**

1. Pull ribs from fridge 20 min before cooking to take the chill off.
2. Air fry at 450°F for 8 minutes. No flipping needed.
3. Cook jasmine rice (rice cooker or stovetop per package).
4. Plate ribs over rice with kimchi on the side and fruit.

**Birria Tacos + Sandia Agua Fresca** *(Friday, June 12)*
Total time: ~1 hr 15 min (Instant Pot) · Approx calories: ~680 per adult

> Birria is deeply, almost obscenely flavorful: chuck roast braised under pressure in a blended sauce of rehydrated guajillo and ancho chiles, chipotles in adobo, tomatoes, garlic, and warm spices. The broth becomes the consommé for dipping — you dip the raw tortilla in the orange fat layer before pan-frying, which gives quesabirria tacos their signature crispy, broth-tinged exterior. The watermelon agua fresca — five minutes to blend — cools everything down.

**Ingredients Used**

- Chuck roast (2 lbs, cut into 5–6 chunks)
- Dried guajillo chiles (4 pods)
- Dried ancho chiles (4 pods)
- Chipotles in adobo (2 chiles from 1 small can)
- Roma tomatoes (2)
- Garlic (4 cloves)
- Salt (1 tsp), dried oregano (1 tsp), ground cumin (1 tsp)
- Bay leaves (2)
- White vinegar (1 tbsp)
- Corn tortillas
- Oaxaca cheese (shredded)
- Cilantro, white onion (diced fine), lime wedges
- Watermelon (other half from Wednesday), lime juice, sugar (2 tbsp), cold water (4 cups)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Soak dried guajillo and ancho chiles in boiling water 20 min. Drain, reserving ½ cup soaking water. Remove stems and seeds.
2. Blend chiles with roma tomatoes, garlic cloves, chipotles in adobo, salt, oregano, cumin, white vinegar, and ½ cup soaking water until completely smooth.
3. Season chuck roast chunks with salt. Heat the Instant Pot on Sauté mode (or a heavy skillet). Sear beef on all sides until browned, ~3 min per side. Transfer chunks to Instant Pot.
4. Pour blended chile sauce over the beef. Add bay leaves. Seal and pressure cook on HIGH for 45 min. Natural release 10–15 min. Discard bay leaves.
5. Lift out beef chunks and shred with two forks. Return shredded meat to the caldo to soak and stay warm.
6. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high. Skim some of the orange fat from the top of the caldo into the pan. Dip a corn tortilla into the caldo (the orange fat layer), lay it on the hot skillet. Add a scoop of shredded beef and a handful of Oaxaca cheese to one half. Fold, press firmly, cook 2–3 min per side until crispy and cheese is melted.
7. Serve tacos immediately with a small bowl of consommé alongside for dipping. Top with chopped white onion, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
8. Agua fresca: Blend watermelon chunks until smooth, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve. Stir in lime juice, 2 tbsp sugar, and 4 cups cold water. Chill and serve alongside dinner.
