**� Ethiopian Beef Tibs** *(SUNDAY, April 19)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~480 per adult

> A staple of Ethiopian restaurants — tender beef cubes sautéed fast over high heat with onion, jalapeño, rosemary, and berbere until slightly charred at the edges. Served over rice (injera is traditional — grab it from an Ethiopian restaurant if you can, otherwise rice works great).

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef, sirloin or eye round (1.5 lbs, cut into 1-inch cubes)
- Yellow onion (1 large, sliced)
- Jalapeños (2, sliced into rings — seeds in for heat, out for mild)
- Tomato, plum (2, diced) — or 1 Roma
- Fresh rosemary (2 sprigs) — or 1 tsp dried
- Berbere spice blend (1½ tsp) — check 1426 St Johns or Brooklyn Fare international aisle
- Butter (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Jasmine rice — for serving; pantry
- Oranges (1 per person, sliced) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Start the rice:** Cook rice per package directions before starting the tibs.
2. **Sear the beef:** Pat beef cubes dry and season with salt, pepper, and berbere. Heat oil in a heavy skillet or cast iron over high heat until smoking. Add beef in a single layer — don't crowd. Sear without stirring 2–3 min until browned. Flip and sear another 1–2 min. Remove and set aside.
3. **Sauté the veg:** In the same pan over medium-high, add butter. Sauté onion slices until softened and slightly charred, ~4 min. Add garlic, jalapeño, and rosemary. Cook 1–2 min more.
4. **Combine:** Add tomatoes and the seared beef back to the pan. Toss everything together and cook 2–3 min until beef is cooked through and everything is nicely mixed. Taste and adjust salt.
5. Serve over rice. Oranges sliced on the side.

> **Tip:** High heat and not overcrowding the pan is what gives Tibs its characteristic char. If you have a small pan, sear the beef in two batches rather than steaming it.

**🌮 Mexican Ceviche Tostadas** *(MONDAY, April 20)*
Total time: ~30 min active + 30 min curing · Approx calories: ~350 per adult

> Classic Mexican ceviche — shrimp "cooked" in fresh lime juice, tossed with crisp vegetables and served on crunchy tostadas with sliced avocado. Light, fast, zero actual cooking required.

**Ingredients Used**

- Shrimp, medium (~1.5 lbs, peeled & deveined) — buy fresh or thawed
- Limes (6–7) — for juice (~½ cup needed to fully submerge the shrimp)
- Red onion (½, finely diced)
- Jalapeño (1–2, minced, seeds removed for less heat)
- Tomatoes, plum (3, diced small)
- Cucumber (½ medium, diced small)
- Avocado (2, sliced)
- Cilantro (1 good handful, chopped)
- Tostada shells (8–10 shells)
- Mango (1, sliced) — fresh fruit side
- Salt — pantry
- Cholula or hot sauce — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Prep the shrimp:** Chop raw shrimp into ½-inch pieces. Place in a bowl and pour enough fresh lime juice to fully submerge (~½ cup, from 6–7 limes). Cover and refrigerate 30 min. The shrimp will turn pink and opaque — "cooked" by the acid. Drain off most of the lime juice.
2. **Mix:** Add red onion, jalapeño, tomatoes, and cucumber. Toss to combine. Season with salt and a dash of Cholula.
3. **Serve:** Pile ceviche onto tostada shells. Fan avocado slices on top. Finish with cilantro. Sliced mango on the side.

> **Tip:** Taste after mixing and adjust salt and lime. If it's too tart, add a pinch of sugar. Ceviche holds well in the fridge for 1 day — make extra.

**🧀 Mac & Cheese** *(TUESDAY FAST, April 21)*
Total time: ~15 min · Approx calories: ~430 per adult

> Boxed mac & cheese — Kraft or store brand. Peas stirred in at the end for some green. Done in 15 minutes, no drama.

**Ingredients Used**

- Boxed mac & cheese (2 boxes) — Kraft or store brand
- Frozen peas (1 cup, stirred in while draining) — pantry/freezer
- Butter (per box directions) — pantry
- Lactaid Whole Milk (per box directions) — pantry/weekly
- Bananas (from the weekly bunch) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. Boil pasta per box directions. About 1 minute before done, add frozen peas to the pot. Drain together.
2. Return to pot. Add butter, milk, and cheese powder per box directions. Stir until creamy.
3. Serve immediately. Bananas on the side.

**� Maggi Beef Ribs** *(WEDNESDAY KIDS NIGHT, April 22)*
Total time: ~1 hr 15 min (mostly hands-off) · Approx calories: ~520 per adult

> The easiest ribs you'll make. Beef ribs simmered in water with Maggi seasoning and onions until fall-off-the-bone tender. Serve over rice with kimchi and oranges on the side. Kids can help set it up.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef ribs (~2–2.5 lbs) — bone-in short ribs or back ribs
- Yellow onion (1, halved or quartered)
- Maggi seasoning sauce (2–3 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Long-grain or jasmine rice (2 cups) — pantry
- Kimchi (1 jar) — side
- Oranges (1 per person) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Set up the pot:** Place ribs in a pot. Cover with water. Add halved onion, Maggi seasoning, salt, and black pepper.
2. **Simmer:** Bring to a boil, skim any foam, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook 60–75 min until the meat is tender and pulling away from the bone.
3. **Cook the rice:** Start rice about 25 min before ribs are done.
4. **Serve:** Ribs over rice. Ladle a little of the broth over the top. Kimchi and sliced oranges on the side.

> **Kids note:** They can set the table, serve themselves kimchi, and peel the oranges. Low-effort night for everyone.

**� Al Pastor Street Tacos** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP, April 24)*
Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~500 per adult

> Ground beef or Impossible meat in a quick pastor marinade — chili, achiote, cumin, pineapple juice — with fresh pineapple chunks, cilantro, onion, and salsa on corn tortillas. Teens cook the meat, everyone builds their own.

**Ingredients Used**

- Ground beef, 85/15 (1.5 lbs) — or Impossible ground meat
- Corn tortillas (1 pack, 12–16 ct)
- Yellow onion (½, finely diced) — topping
- Cilantro (handful, chopped) — topping
- Pineapple (½ fresh, diced small) — in the meat + topping
- Salsa (1 jar) — pantry or fresh
- Achiote paste or powder (1 tbsp) — pantry/spice rack; or sub extra chili powder + smoked paprika
- Cumin (1 tsp) — pantry
- Chili powder (1 tsp) — pantry
- Garlic powder (1 tsp) — pantry
- Apple cider vinegar or lime juice (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp) — pantry

**Cooking Overview (Teen Instructions)**

1. **Mix the marinade:** In a bowl, combine achiote, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Toss the ground meat in it.
2. **Cook the meat:** Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high. Add the seasoned meat and break it up. Cook 8–10 min until browned throughout. In the last 2 min, fold in half the diced pineapple and let it caramelize slightly.
3. **Warm the tortillas:** Heat corn tortillas one at a time directly on the gas burner (15 sec per side) or in a dry skillet.
4. **Build the tacos:** Meat in the tortilla. Pineapple chunks, cilantro, diced onion, salsa on top.

> **Teen note:** The achiote is what gives pastor its red color and earthy flavor — don't skip it. Check the pantry/spice rack first; if there's none, use smoked paprika + extra chili powder.
