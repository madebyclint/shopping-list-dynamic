**� Ethiopian Beef Tibs** *(SUNDAY, April 19)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~480 per adult

> A staple of Ethiopian restaurants — tender beef cubes sautéed fast over high heat with onion, jalapeño, rosemary, and berbere until slightly charred at the edges. Served over rice (injera is traditional — grab it from an Ethiopian restaurant if you can, otherwise rice works great).

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef, sirloin or eye round (1.5 lbs, cut into 1-inch cubes)
- Yellow onion (1 large, sliced)
- Jalapeños (2, sliced into rings — seeds in for heat, out for mild)
- Tomato, plum (2, diced) — or 1 Roma
- Fresh rosemary (2 sprigs) — or 1 tsp dried
- Berbere spice blend (1½ tsp) — check 1426 St Johns or Brooklyn Fare international aisle
- Butter (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Jasmine rice — for serving; pantry
- Oranges (1 per person, sliced) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Start the rice:** Cook rice per package directions before starting the tibs.
2. **Sear the beef:** Pat beef cubes dry and season with salt, pepper, and berbere. Heat oil in a heavy skillet or cast iron over high heat until smoking. Add beef in a single layer — don't crowd. Sear without stirring 2–3 min until browned. Flip and sear another 1–2 min. Remove and set aside.
3. **Sauté the veg:** In the same pan over medium-high, add butter. Sauté onion slices until softened and slightly charred, ~4 min. Add garlic, jalapeño, and rosemary. Cook 1–2 min more.
4. **Combine:** Add tomatoes and the seared beef back to the pan. Toss everything together and cook 2–3 min until beef is cooked through and everything is nicely mixed. Taste and adjust salt.
5. Serve over rice. Oranges sliced on the side.

> **Tip:** High heat and not overcrowding the pan is what gives Tibs its characteristic char. If you have a small pan, sear the beef in two batches rather than steaming it.

**🌮 Mexican Ceviche Tostadas** *(MONDAY, April 20)*
Total time: ~30 min active + 30 min curing · Approx calories: ~350 per adult

> Classic Mexican ceviche — shrimp "cooked" in fresh lime juice, tossed with crisp vegetables and served on crunchy tostadas with sliced avocado. Light, fast, zero actual cooking required.

**Ingredients Used**

- Shrimp, medium (~1.5 lbs, peeled & deveined) — buy fresh or thawed
- Limes (6–7) — for juice (~½ cup needed to fully submerge the shrimp)
- Red onion (½, finely diced)
- Jalapeño (1–2, minced, seeds removed for less heat)
- Tomatoes, plum (3, diced small)
- Cucumber (½ medium, diced small)
- Avocado (2, sliced)
- Cilantro (1 good handful, chopped)
- Tostada shells (8–10 shells)
- Mango (1, sliced) — fresh fruit side
- Salt — pantry
- Cholula or hot sauce — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Prep the shrimp:** Chop raw shrimp into ½-inch pieces. Place in a bowl and pour enough fresh lime juice to fully submerge (~½ cup, from 6–7 limes). Cover and refrigerate 30 min. The shrimp will turn pink and opaque — "cooked" by the acid. Drain off most of the lime juice.
2. **Mix:** Add red onion, jalapeño, tomatoes, and cucumber. Toss to combine. Season with salt and a dash of Cholula.
3. **Serve:** Pile ceviche onto tostada shells. Fan avocado slices on top. Finish with cilantro. Sliced mango on the side.

> **Tip:** Taste after mixing and adjust salt and lime. If it's too tart, add a pinch of sugar. Ceviche holds well in the fridge for 1 day — make extra.

Total time: ~15 min · Approx calories: ~430 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Boxed mac & cheese (2 boxes) — Kraft or store brand
- Frozen peas (1 cup, stirred in while draining) — pantry/freezer
- Butter (per box directions) — pantry
- Lactaid Whole Milk (per box directions) — pantry/weekly

**🍗 Nigerian Jollof Rice with Chicken** *(WEDNESDAY, April 22)*
Total time: ~45 min · Approx calories: ~560 per adult

> The West African party rice — long-grain rice cooked in a blended tomato-pepper base until smoky and deeply flavored. Chicken thighs go in first, perfuming the pot. Don't open the lid too early.

**Ingredients Used**

- Chicken thighs, bone-in (~3 lbs / 6 pieces) — or boneless if preferred
- Long-grain rice (2 cups) — or jasmine
- Plum tomatoes (3, whole) — for the base
- Red bell pepper (1, roughly chopped) — for the base
- Scotch bonnet or habanero (½, seeds in if you like heat) — for the base
- Yellow onion (1 large) — half for blending, half sliced for sautéing
- Tomato paste (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Chicken bouillon cubes (2) or chicken stock (1 cup) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (3 tbsp) — pantry
- Garlic powder, onion powder, curry powder (1 tsp each) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Mixed frozen veg (1 cup, stirred in at the end) — pantry/freezer
- Pineapple (½ fresh, sliced) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Blend the base:** Blend plum tomatoes, red bell pepper, scotch bonnet, and half the onion until smooth. Set aside.
2. **Sear the chicken:** Season chicken with salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, curry powder. Heat oil in a heavy pot over medium-high. Brown chicken pieces skin-side down, 3–4 min per side. Remove and set aside.
3. **Build the base:** In the same pot, sauté sliced onion 2 min. Add tomato paste and stir 1 min. Pour in the blended tomato mixture. Cook over medium-high, stirring, until reduced and deep red, ~10 min. Season with bouillon and salt.
4. **Cook the rice:** Rinse rice and add to the pot. Stir to coat in the tomato base. Nestle chicken pieces back in. Add just enough water or stock to barely cover the rice (~2 cups). Bring to a boil, then reduce to lowest heat. Cover tightly.
5. **The steam phase:** Cook covered 25–30 min. Don't lift the lid. Let it catch slightly at the bottom — that's the "party jollof" crust. Stir in frozen veg in the last 5 min.
6. Serve with pineapple slices.

> **Tip:** That slightly charred bottom layer is intentional and prized — it's the smoky signature of Nigerian Jollof. If it's just starting to smell toasted (not burnt), you're doing it right.

**🌯 Beef & Veggie Burrito Bowls** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP, April 24)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

> Same move, no rolling required. Beef and roasted peppers over rice, beans and cheese on top. Teens build their own bowls.

**Ingredients Used**

- Ground beef (1.5 lbs, 85/15)
- Green bell pepper (1, sliced)
- Red bell pepper (1, sliced)
- Yellow onion (1, sliced)
- Black beans, canned (1 can, drained and rinsed)
- Shredded Mexican cheese blend (1 bag, 8 oz)
- Jasmine rice (2 cups) — pantry
- Olive oil (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Cumin, garlic powder, chili powder (1 tsp each) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Salsa, sour cream — for serving; pantry
- Oranges (1 per person) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview (Teen Instructions)**

1. **Start the rice:** Cook jasmine rice per package directions.
2. **Preheat oven:** Set to 425°F.
3. **Roast the veg:** Toss bell peppers and onion with olive oil, salt, pepper. Spread on a sheet pan. Roast 20–22 min, flipping halfway, until charred at the edges.
4. **Brown the beef:** In a large skillet over medium-high, cook ground beef, breaking it up, until fully browned (no pink) — about 8–10 min. Drain fat. Season with cumin, garlic powder, chili powder, and salt.
5. **Warm the beans:** Drain and rinse black beans. Add to the beef skillet and stir until warmed through, 2 min.
6. **Build the bowls:** Rice on the bottom. Beef-bean mix on top. Roasted veg on the side. Shredded cheese over everything. Salsa and sour cream to finish.
7. Oranges sliced on the side.

> **Teen note:** Don't skip draining the beef fat — pour it into an old can, not down the sink.
