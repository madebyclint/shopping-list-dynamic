**� Arroz Caldo** *(SUNDAY BRUNCH, April 26 — 7 people)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~380 per adult

> Classic Filipino rice porridge — congee-style, silky and thick, loaded with ginger and finished with crispy fried garlic and scallions. This is the cheat version: rotisserie chicken shredded in, cooked risotto-style with Better Than Bouillon until thick like porridge. Scales up easily. Feeds a crowd with very little fuss.

**Ingredients Used**

- Rotisserie chicken (1 large, deboned and shredded)
- Sushi rice (4 cups, dry — scaled up for 7 people)
- Fresh ginger (lots — ~3–4 oz, thinly sliced, not minced)
- Green onions (3 bunches — whites for cooking, greens for garnish)
- Better Than Bouillon, beef or chicken (2–3 tsp dissolved in hot water, to taste)
- Water (~8–10 cups, added gradually)
- Vegetable oil (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Fish sauce — pantry

**Garnishes** *(set out on the table)*

- Fried garlic (store-bought jar/bag)
- Peanuts, roasted (handful per bowl)
- Kecap manis / sweet soy sauce (drizzle) — *(1426 St Johns or Brooklyn Fare; skip if unavailable)*
- Salt & vinegar chips (from weekly bags — crumble over the top or eat on the side)
- Extra green onion greens, sliced
- Fish sauce and/or ketchup alongside

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Debone the chicken.** Pull apart the rotisserie chicken and shred the meat into bite-sized pieces. Save the skin if you want to crisp it — optional garnish.
2. **Sauté the ginger.** Heat oil in a large heavy pot over medium. Add the thinly sliced ginger and cook, stirring, until fragrant and beginning to soften, ~3–4 min. Be generous — this is the backbone of the flavor.
3. **Add the scallion whites.** Slice the white parts off all the green onion bunches and add to the pot. Cook another 2–3 min until softened. Reserve greens for garnish.
4. **Add the rice.** Pour in 4 cups dry sushi rice. Stir to coat in the oil and aromatics.
5. **Cook risotto-style.** Dissolve Better Than Bouillon in 2 cups hot water and add to the pot. Stir. As the liquid absorbs, add water 1 cup at a time, stirring frequently. Keep heat at medium. Continue until the rice is thick, creamy, and porridge-like — about 20–22 min total, using roughly 8–10 cups liquid.
6. **Taste and adjust.** Add more bouillon for depth, more ginger for punch, salt as needed.
7. **Add the chicken.** Fold in shredded rotisserie chicken and heat through, ~3 min.
8. **Serve.** Ladle into bowls. Top with fried garlic, sliced green onion greens, and peanuts. Drizzle with kecap manis and fish sauce. Crumble salt & vinegar chips alongside.

> **Note:** Sushi rice breaks down into a creamier, thicker porridge than jasmine rice — ideal for Arroz Caldo. Don't rush the risotto step; slow liquid addition is what gives it that silky consistency.

> **Fried garlic from scratch:** Thinly slice 6–8 garlic cloves. Fry in ¼ cup oil over medium heat, stirring constantly, until golden and crispy, ~3–4 min. Watch closely — they burn fast. Drain on paper towels.

**�🍆 Filipino Pinakbet with Beef** *(SUNDAY, April 26)*
Total time: ~40 min · Approx calories: ~490 per adult

> Pinakbet is a beloved Filipino vegetable stew — traditionally made with pork and bagoong (fermented shrimp paste). This version swaps in beef chunks and fish sauce, a widely used home-cooking substitute. Eggplant, chayote, butternut squash, okra, and green beans braised with tomatoes, ginger, and garlic, served over rice.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef stew chunks (~1.5 lbs, cut into 1–2 inch pieces)
- Eggplant (2 medium, cut into 1-inch chunks)
- Chayote (2, peeled and cut into 1-inch chunks)
- Butternut squash (1 small, peeled and cubed ~1 inch, ~2 cups)
- Okra (~½ lb, trimmed, halved)
- Green beans (~½ lb, trimmed, cut into 2-inch pieces)
- Tomatoes, plum (3, diced)
- Yellow onion (1 medium, sliced)
- Fish sauce (2–3 tbsp) — pantry; replaces traditional bagoong
- Garlic (4 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Ginger (1 tbsp, minced) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Jasmine rice — pantry
- Oranges (1 per person) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Start the rice.** Cook rice per package directions.
2. **Brown the beef.** Heat oil in a wide pot or large skillet over medium-high. Season beef with salt and pepper. Brown in batches, ~3 min per side. Remove and set aside.
3. **Build the base.** In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic over medium 2–3 min until softened. Add ginger and cook 1 min more. Add diced tomatoes and fish sauce. Stir and cook 2–3 min until tomatoes begin to break down.
4. **Add the harder vegetables.** Return beef to the pot. Add butternut squash and chayote. Pour in ½ cup water. Stir, cover, and simmer 10–12 min until squash begins to soften.
5. **Add the tender vegetables.** Add eggplant, okra, and green beans. Fold gently to combine. Cover and cook 8–10 min until all vegetables are tender and beef is cooked through. Taste and adjust salt.
6. Serve over rice. Oranges on the side.

> **Note:** Traditional Pinakbet uses bagoong for deeply savory, fermented depth — fish sauce is a great substitute, widely used across Filipino home kitchens. If the stew tastes flat, add another tbsp fish sauce.

> **Tip:** Don't stir aggressively once the eggplant and okra go in — they'll break apart. Use a gentle fold or just shake the pot instead.

**🍋 Chickpea Sopa de Lima** *(MONDAY, April 27)*
Total time: ~35 min · Approx calories: ~380 per adult

> Sopa de Lima is a bright, tangy soup from the Yucatán Peninsula. The base is built on charred tomatoes, onion, and jalapeño blended into the broth, then finished with a generous squeeze of lime. Chickpeas make it hearty and pantry-friendly. Tortilla strips on top for crunch.

**Ingredients Used**

- Canned chickpeas (2 cans, 15 oz each, drained and rinsed) — pantry
- Tomatoes, plum (3, halved)
- Yellow onion (1, quartered)
- Jalapeños (2 — 1 for charring, 1 diced raw for fresh heat)
- Limes (4, juiced — ~⅓ cup)
- Cilantro (1 handful, chopped)
- Chicken broth (4 cups) — pantry
- Cumin (1 tsp) — pantry
- Garlic (3 cloves) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt — pantry
- Tortilla chips (1–2 handfuls, crushed) — from weekly chip bags
- Pineapple (1, sliced) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Char the base.** Heat a dry skillet or cast iron over high heat. Place tomato halves, quartered onion, and 1 whole jalapeño cut-side down. Char 4–5 min until dark and blistered. Flip and char the other side 2–3 min.
2. **Blend.** Transfer charred veg to a blender with 1 cup broth. Blend until smooth.
3. **Build the soup.** Heat oil in a pot over medium. Add garlic and the raw diced jalapeño, cook 1–2 min. Pour in the blended tomato mixture. Cook 3–4 min, stirring.
4. **Add broth and chickpeas.** Pour in remaining 3 cups broth and drained chickpeas. Add cumin. Bring to a simmer and cook 15 min to meld flavors.
5. **Finish.** Remove from heat. Stir in lime juice. Taste and adjust salt and lime — the soup should be bright and tangy.
6. Serve topped with crushed tortilla chips and cilantro. Pineapple slices on the side.

> **Tip:** Charring the tomatoes and onion is the step that makes this soup — it adds smoky depth that makes it taste like it simmered all day. Don't skip it.

**🍔 Hamburgers with Watermelon** *(TUESDAY FAST, April 28)*
Total time: ~15 min · Approx calories: ~520 per adult

> Pre-made beef patties on a flat pan or grill pan — melted cheddar, lettuce, tomato, onion on toasted buns. Cold watermelon wedges on the side. Fast Tuesday, done.

**Ingredients Used**

- Pre-made beef burger patties (1 pack, 4–6 ct)
- Burger buns (1 pack, 4–6 ct)
- Cheddar, shredded or sliced (4 portions) — from weekly cheddar bag
- Lettuce (4 leaves) — from salad mix or lettuce head
- Tomato, plum (1, sliced into rounds)
- Yellow onion (¼, thinly sliced) — optional
- Watermelon (1 small seedless, sliced into wedges) — fresh fruit side
- Ketchup, mustard — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil or butter (for pan) — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Cook the patties.** Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high. Brush lightly with oil. Place patties — cook ~3–4 min without pressing down. Flip. Add cheddar on top and cook 2–3 min more until cooked through.
2. **Toast the buns.** In the last minute, place bun halves face-down in the pan or under the broiler until lightly golden.
3. **Assemble.** LTO on the bottom bun. Patty with melted cheese. Condiments. Top bun.
4. Watermelon wedges on the side.

> **Tip:** Don't press the patties — you'll squeeze out the juice. Let them sit undisturbed for a good sear.

**🦃 Cheesy Turkey Pasta Skillet** *(WEDNESDAY, April 29)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~470 per adult

> From-scratch Hamburger Helper — ground turkey browned with onion and garlic, then pasta shells added directly and simmered in beef broth and canned tomatoes until thick and saucy. Shredded cheddar melted over the top. Garden salad and apple slices on the side.

**Ingredients Used**

- Ground turkey (1 lb)
- Pasta shells (2 cups / ~½ lb) — pantry; note if running low
- Canned diced tomatoes (1 can, 14 oz) — pantry
- Beef broth (2 cups) — pantry
- Yellow onion (½, diced)
- Shredded cheddar (1½ cups / ~6 oz)
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Paprika (1 tsp) — pantry
- Dried oregano (½ tsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Salad mix or head of lettuce (1) — garden salad side
- Apples (4–5) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Brown the turkey.** Heat oil in a large wide skillet over medium-high. Add turkey and break it apart. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Cook 5–6 min until browned. Add onion and garlic, cook 2–3 min more.
2. **Add liquid and pasta.** Pour in broth, canned tomatoes (with their liquid), and oregano. Stir in dry pasta shells. Bring to a boil.
3. **Simmer.** Reduce to a medium simmer. Cook uncovered 12–14 min, stirring every few minutes, until pasta is tender and the liquid has thickened into a clingy sauce.
4. **Melt the cheese.** Reduce heat to low. Sprinkle shredded cheddar over the top. Cover and let melt 2 min.
5. Serve from the skillet. Garden salad on the side (bag or head of lettuce with oil and vinegar). Apples sliced alongside.

> **Note:** Taste after the pasta finishes cooking — it may want a pinch more salt or a dash of hot sauce. The pasta releases starch as it cooks, which naturally thickens the sauce.

**🌽 Esquites with Beef Hot Dogs** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP, April 30)*
Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~450 per adult

> Esquites are Mexican street corn in a bowl — frozen corn heated in a pot, tossed with homemade mayo, lime, chili powder, and crumbled cotija. Hot dogs cooked separately and served on buns on the side — a full meal alongside the corn bowls. Teens run this one start to finish.

**Ingredients Used**

- Uncured beef hot dogs (1 pack, 8 ct)
- Hot dog buns (1 pack, 8 ct)
- Frozen corn (1 lb bag)
- Mayo — make from scratch: 2 egg yolks + 1 cup avocado oil, immersion blender (eggs and avocado oil on hand)
- Limes (2, juiced)
- Cotija cheese (¼ cup, crumbled) — pantry (on hand)
- Chili powder (1 tsp) — pantry
- Cilantro (a few sprigs, chopped)
- Butter (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt — pantry
- Mango (2, sliced) — fresh fruit side

**Cooking Overview**

*(Teens make this — step by step)*

1. **Separate the yolks.** Crack an egg on the edge of a bowl. Hold the shell halves over the bowl and transfer the yolk back and forth between them, letting the white fall into the bowl below. Drop the yolk into a tall narrow container. Repeat for the second egg. A little white in the container is fine — small amounts won't affect the mayo. What ruins it is water or a broken yolk (a yellow streak in the white means the yolk broke; still usable but crack carefully).
2. **Make the mayo.** Pour 1 cup avocado oil on top of the yolks — don't stir. Place the immersion blender at the bottom and blend without moving for ~10 seconds until it turns white and thick. Then slowly pull the blender up. Season with salt and a squeeze of lime. Set aside.
3. **Heat the corn.** Pour frozen corn into a pot with a small knob of butter and a splash of water. Cover and heat over medium until warmed through, ~5 min. Drain any excess liquid. Transfer to a large bowl.
4. **Cook the hot dogs.** In a pan over medium-high with a little butter, cook hot dogs whole, turning until browned on all sides, ~4 min. Serve in buns on the side — these are eaten separately, not in the corn bowl.
5. **Dress the corn.** Add mayo, lime juice, chili powder, and a pinch of salt to the corn bowl. Toss to coat. Taste and adjust.
6. **Serve.** Spoon esquites into bowls. Crumble cotija over everything. Finish with cilantro. Hot dogs in buns on the side. Mango slices.

> **Tip for teens — mayo:** Keep the blender at the bottom for the first 10 seconds without moving it — this is what emulsifies it. Only start pulling up slowly once it turns white and creamy. If it stays liquid, it broke; start over with fresh yolks. A little egg white is fine — water is not, so dry the container first.
