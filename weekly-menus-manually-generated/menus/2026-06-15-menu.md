## Deeper View

**Beef Burgers** *(Monday, June 15)*
Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~650 per adult

> Classic smash-style or formed patties — your call. The goal is fast, satisfying, and done before 6:30pm. Brioche buns toasted in the pan. Standard build: lettuce, tomato, onion, cheese, ketchup, mustard. Oranges on the side.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef patties (from freezer)
- Brioche burger buns
- Cheddar or American cheese
- Lettuce, tomato, onion (from fridge)
- Ketchup, mustard, mayo (pantry condiments)
- Oranges (sliced, on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pull patties from freezer if not already defrosted; thaw in fridge overnight or use microwave defrost.
2. Season patties with salt and pepper just before cooking.
3. Heat a cast iron or heavy pan over medium-high heat. Cook patties 3–4 min per side for medium (longer for well done).
4. Add cheese in the last 1 min; cover with a lid or foil to melt.
5. Toast buns cut-side down in residual pan fat, 1–2 min.
6. Build and serve with sliced oranges on the side.

**Quesadillas** *(Tuesday, June 16 — FAST)*
Total time: ≤20 min · Approx calories: ~480 per adult

> Fast night. Flour tortillas, cheese, and whatever filling is in the fridge — leftover proteins, a few veg, whatever makes sense. Pan-fried until the exterior is golden and the cheese is fully melted. Mangoes on the side.

**Ingredients Used**

- Flour tortillas (large burrito-size)
- Shredded cheese (cheddar, jack, or a mix)
- Filling of choice (leftover chicken, beans, sautéed veg, etc.)
- Butter or oil (for pan)
- Sour cream or salsa (for dipping, if on hand)
- Mangoes (sliced, on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat; add a thin layer of butter or oil.
2. Place one tortilla in the pan. Layer cheese and filling over half (fold-style) or full surface (then top with a second tortilla).
3. Cook 2–3 min until the bottom is golden; flip and cook another 1–2 min until cheese is melted.
4. Slice into wedges. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
5. Serve with sliced mangoes on the side.

**Eggs, Tofu & Soba** *(Wednesday, June 17)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~480 per adult

> A clean, gentle weeknight bowl. Soba noodles in a warm soy-mirin broth, a 7-minute soft-boiled egg halved on top, pan-fried tofu for protein, and fresh green onions and ginger for aromatics. Melon on the side keeps it light.

**Ingredients Used**

- Soba noodles (1 bundle per person)
- Firm or extra-firm tofu (1 block, pressed and sliced)
- Eggs (1–2 per person)
- Green onions (sliced thin, for garnish)
- Fresh ginger (1-inch knob, grated or thin-sliced)
- Soy sauce (3–4 tbsp)
- Mirin (2 tbsp)
- Sesame oil (1 tsp, optional)
- Dashi powder or broth (pantry) — or just a light soy broth
- Melon (sliced, on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Boil a pot of water; cook soba noodles per package (usually 4–5 min). Rinse well under cold water to remove starch; drain and set aside.
2. Soft-boil eggs: bring water to a boil, lower eggs in gently, cook exactly 7 min, transfer to ice water, peel when cool.
3. Press tofu between paper towels; cut into ½-inch slabs. Pan-fry in a non-stick or cast iron with a little oil over medium-high heat, 3–4 min per side until golden. Season with a splash of soy sauce in the last minute.
4. Make broth: combine 2 cups water (or dashi broth), soy sauce, mirin, and a few slices of ginger in a small pot; bring to a simmer.
5. Divide soba into bowls. Ladle warm broth over. Top with tofu, halved soft-boiled egg, sliced green onions, and grated ginger.
6. Serve with sliced melon on the side.

**Sardines & Rice** *(Thursday, June 18 — TEEN PREP)*
Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~420 per adult

> Teen-proof and legitimately tasty. Jasmine rice in the rice cooker, canned sardines (in olive oil or tomato sauce) served warm or at room temp, green onions on top, soy sauce or hot sauce on the side. Melon alongside. Everything except the green onions and melon is already in the pantry.

> 💡 **Teen note:** Start the rice cooker first — it takes the longest. Open the sardines, warm if you want. Slice green onions. Done.

**Ingredients Used**

- Canned sardines (2–3 tins — in olive oil or tomato sauce)
- Jasmine rice (2 cups dry, from pantry)
- Green onions (sliced thin, from Wednesday's bunch)
- Soy sauce (for drizzling, optional)
- Hot sauce (optional)
- Melon (sliced, from Wednesday's purchase)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Rinse and cook jasmine rice in the rice cooker (standard 2:1 water ratio, ~20 min).
2. Open sardine tins. If warming: place sardines in a small pan over low heat 2–3 min until just heated through.
3. Slice green onions.
4. Serve sardines over rice, topped with green onions. Set out soy sauce and hot sauce on the table.
5. Serve with sliced melon.
