**🥯 Salmon Lox Bagel Sandwiches** *(TUESDAY FAST — March 24)*
Total time: ~10 min · Approx calories: ~460 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Smoked salmon / lox (~3–4 oz per person, ~12–16 oz total)
- Cream cheese, plain (8 oz block)
- Bagels, everything or assorted (1 per person — 4–6 bagels)
- Red onion (¼, very thinly sliced)
- Cucumber (1, thinly sliced)
- Tomato (1 large, thinly sliced)
- Capers (2 tbsp, drained)
- Fresh dill (2–3 sprigs per bagel)
- Black pepper — pantry
- Orange slices (served on the side — from weekly assortment)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Slice bagels and toast lightly if preferred.
2. Spread a generous layer of cream cheese on each half.
3. Layer with cucumber slices, tomato, and thin red onion rings.
4. Drape 3–4 oz of lox across each sandwich. Add capers and a few sprigs of fresh dill.
5. Crack fresh black pepper over the top. Serve open-faced or closed with orange slices on the side.

**🍖 Simple Maggi Beef Ribs** *(Wednesday, March 25)*
Total time: ~45–50 min · Approx calories: ~620 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef short ribs, bone-in (~3 lbs, all beef — for Julian)
- Broccoli (1 head, cut into florets)
- Jasmine rice (cooked base) — pantry
- Pineapple chunks (served on the side)
- Salt, black pepper, neutral oil — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pat ribs dry, season with salt and black pepper, and sear in a little oil over medium-high heat until browned on both sides.
2. Cover and finish cooking over medium-low heat until tender, turning once midway. Add a splash of water if needed to prevent sticking.
3. While ribs cook, steam or saute broccoli until just tender-crisp and season lightly.
4. Serve ribs with rice, broccoli, and pineapple on the side.

**🧀 Grilled Cheese & Tomato Soup** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP — March 26)*
Total time: ~20–25 min · Approx calories: ~550 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Bread, sandwich white (2 slices per person — 8 slices total)
- Muenster cheese, sliced (3–4 slices per sandwich)
- Campbell's Tomato Soup (2 cans)
- Lactaid milk (½ cup — to make soup creamier) — from weekly carton
- Salt, black pepper — pantry

**Cooking Overview — Teen Steps**

1. **Open the soup** — pour 2 cans of tomato soup into a medium pot. Add about 2/3 cup milk (or to desired texture). Stir together and heat over medium, stirring often. Don't let it boil hard.
2. **Prep the sandwiches** — lay out 2 bread slices per person. Add 3–4 slices of muenster per sandwich and close.
3. **Cook the sandwiches** — heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Toast both sides until golden and the cheese is fully melted, pressing lightly with a spatula.
4. **Taste the soup** — add a pinch of salt and black pepper. If it seems too thick, add a splash more milk or water and stir.
5. **Serve** — cut sandwiches diagonally. Ladle soup into bowls.

**🥩 Air-Fryer Corned Beef Brisket** *(Monday, March 23)*
Total time: ~35 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Corned beef brisket, flat cut (~3 lbs, with spice packet)
- Carrots, Cal Organic (1 bag, roasted for side salad)
- Leftover cabbage (no new groceries)
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper, smoked paprika — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F.
2. Score the fat side of the brisket, then season with salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika.
3. Place brisket fat-side up in the basket and cook for 20 minutes.
4. Rest for 10 minutes. Internal temperature should be around 150°F after resting.
5. Slice after resting and serve with leftover cabbage plus roasted carrot salad.

**🥩 Peruvian Lomo Saltado** *(Saturday, March 21 — Carryover)*
Total time: ~25–30 min · Approx calories: ~500 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef, round stir-fry strips or flank steak (1.5 lbs, sliced thin against the grain)
- Tomatoes (2 large, cut into wedges)
- Red onion (1, sliced into strips)
- Scallions (1 bunch, chopped)
- Garlic (2 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Soy sauce (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Rice vinegar (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Cumin (½ tsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil — pantry
- Jasmine rice (cooked) — pantry
- Fresh cilantro or parsley (optional garnish)
- Mango (1, sliced — served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Start the rice first — it takes 15–18 minutes. Use 2:1 water-to-rice ratio, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low.
2. Cut tomatoes into thick wedges (6 per tomato). Slice red onion into strips. Chop scallions into 1-inch pieces.
3. Slice beef thin across the grain — strips about 2–3 inches long and ¼ inch thick. Pat dry and season with salt and pepper.
4. Get a wok or large skillet VERY hot. Add 2 tbsp oil and heat until nearly smoking.
5. Sear beef in a single layer — don't stir for 90 seconds. Toss and cook 1–2 more minutes until browned. Push to the side.
6. Add onion to the empty side — stir fry 1–2 minutes. Add tomato wedges, toss gently 1 minute so they hold their shape.
7. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cumin — toss everything together and let it bubble 30 seconds.
8. Finish with scallions, taste for salt. Plate over rice. Mango slices on the side.

**🥘 West African Goat Peanut Stew (Maafe)** *(Sunday, March 22 — Carryover)*
Total time: ~45–50 min · Approx calories: ~600 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Goat meat, bone-in pieces (~2 lbs) — Ideal Foods or Utica Ave market
- Peanut butter, natural creamy (½ cup)
- Canned crushed or whole tomatoes (1 can) — pantry
- Tomato paste (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Onion, yellow (1 large, diced)
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Ginger, fresh (1-inch piece, grated) — pantry
- Chicken or vegetable broth (1 cup) — pantry
- Cumin (1 tsp), coriander (1 tsp), red pepper flakes or cayenne (to taste) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil — pantry
- Sweet potato (1 large, peeled and cubed)
- Fufu powder (prepared per package) OR jasmine rice — pantry
- Fresh parsley or cilantro (for garnish)
- Pineapple (½ fresh or 1 can chunks — served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pat goat pieces dry and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of cumin. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over high heat. Brown goat in batches, 3–4 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
2. Reduce heat to medium. In the same pot, sauté diced onion 3 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste and cook until it darkens slightly, about 2 minutes.
3. Add canned tomatoes, broth, cumin, coriander, and cayenne. Return browned goat to the pot. Ladle out some hot liquid, whisk peanut butter into it until smooth, then stir it back in (this prevents clumping). Add sweet potato.
4. Cover and simmer over medium-low 30–35 minutes, stirring occasionally, until goat is tender and stew has thickened. Taste and adjust salt and heat.
5. Prepare fufu per package instructions (boil water, whisk in powder vigorously until thick and smooth). Portion into balls — or serve over rice.
6. Ladle stew over fufu or rice. Garnish with parsley or cilantro. Pineapple chunks on the side.
