**🍀 Corned Beef Roast & Cabbage** *(St. Patrick's Day — Monday, March 16)*
Total time: ~90 min (Instant Pot) or 2.5–3 hrs stovetop — plan ahead or start early · Approx calories: ~550 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Corned beef brisket, flat cut (~3 lbs, includes spice packet) — Brooklyn Fare
- Potatoes, white (2 lbs, quartered)
- Carrots (3–4, cut into chunks)
- Cabbage, green (1 head, cut into wedges)
- Onion, yellow (1, quartered)
- Water or beef broth to cover
- Green apples (2, sliced — served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Instant Pot (recommended):** Place corned beef in Instant Pot with the spice packet, quartered onion, and enough water or broth to cover (about 4 cups). Seal and cook on High Pressure for 70 minutes.
2. Quick release, then add potatoes and carrots — seal again and cook 10 more minutes. Add cabbage wedges and either pressure cook 5 more minutes or use the Sauté function to steam until just tender.
3. Slice brisket against the grain. Serve with soda bread and apple slices on the side.

> **Stovetop alternative:** Simmer corned beef covered in cold water with spice packet for 2–2.5 hrs until fork-tender. Add potatoes and carrots the last 40 min, cabbage the last 20 min. Slice against the grain.

**🥪 Reuben Sandwiches** *(TUESDAY FAST — March 17)*
Total time: ~10–15 min · Approx calories: ~600 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Corned beef, deli sliced (~1 lb, from Brooklyn Fare)
- Swiss cheese, sliced (¼ lb)
- Sauerkraut (well-drained)
- Rye bread (8 slices)
- Thousand Island dressing
- Butter (softened)
- Apple slices (served on the side — from same bag as Monday)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Butter one side of each rye bread slice. On the unbuttered side, spread Thousand Island dressing.
2. Layer with deli corned beef, a slice of Swiss, and a spoonful of well-drained sauerkraut. Top with the second bread slice, buttered side facing out.
3. Cook in a skillet or griddle over medium heat, pressing gently, about 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown and cheese is melted. Serve immediately with apple slices on the side.

**Persian Kofta Bowls** *(Wednesday, March 18)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~550 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Ground beef or ground lamb (1.5 lbs)
- Onion, yellow (½, grated)
- Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
- Cumin (1 tsp), coriander (1 tsp), paprika (1 tsp), cinnamon (¼ tsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Cucumber (2, diced)
- Tomatoes (2 medium, diced)
- Red onion (¼, thinly sliced)
- Lemon (1, juiced)
- Olive oil — pantry
- Greek yogurt, plain (1 cup)
- Pita bread (warmed)
- Fresh parsley (for garnish)
- Jasmine rice (optional base — pantry)
- Mandarins (served on the side — from weekly bag)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Mix ground meat with grated onion, minced garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper until combined. Form into oval patties or small logs about 2 inches long.
2. Heat a lightly oiled skillet over medium-high heat. Sear kofta 4–5 minutes per side until cooked through and lightly charred.
3. Make the yogurt sauce: whisk Greek yogurt with 1 minced garlic clove, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.
4. Toss diced cucumber, tomatoes, and red onion with olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper for the salad.
5. Serve kofta over rice or alongside warmed pita, spooned with yogurt sauce and topped with the fresh salad. Finish with chopped parsley. Serve mandarins on the side.

**Peruvian Lomo Saltado** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP — March 19)*
Total time: ~25–30 min · Approx calories: ~500 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef, round stir-fry strips or flank steak (1.5 lbs, sliced thin against the grain)
- Tomatoes (2 large, cut into wedges)
- Red onion (1.5, sliced into strips)
- Scallions (1 bunch, chopped)
- Garlic (2 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Soy sauce (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Rice vinegar (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Cumin (½ tsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil — pantry
- Jasmine rice (cooked) — pantry
- Fresh cilantro or parsley (optional garnish)
- Mango (1, sliced — served on the side)

**Cooking Overview — Teen Steps**

1. **Start the rice first** — it takes 15–18 minutes. Use 2:1 water-to-rice ratio, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low.
2. **Prep the veggies** — cut tomatoes into thick wedges (6 per tomato). Slice red onion into strips. Chop scallions into 1-inch pieces. Mince the garlic.
3. **Slice the beef** thin, cutting across the grain. Strips should be about 2–3 inches long and ¼ inch thick. Pat dry with a paper towel and season with salt and pepper.
4. **Get the pan VERY hot** — use a wok or the biggest skillet you have. Add 2 tbsp oil and heat until almost smoking. High heat is the whole secret to Lomo Saltado.
5. **Sear the beef** in a single layer — don't stir for 90 seconds. Then toss and cook 1–2 more minutes until browned. Push to the side of the pan.
6. **Add garlic and onion** to the empty side — stir fry 1–2 minutes. Add tomato wedges and toss gently 1 minute (they should hold shape, not turn to mush).
7. **Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cumin** — toss everything together and let it bubble 30 seconds.
8. **Finish** with scallions, taste and adjust salt. Plate immediately over rice.

**West African Goat Peanut Stew (Maafe)** *(Friday, March 20)*
Total time: ~45–50 min · Approx calories: ~600 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Goat meat, bone-in pieces (~2 lbs) — Ideal Foods or Utica Ave market
- Peanut butter, natural creamy (½ cup)
- Canned crushed or whole tomatoes (1 can) — pantry
- Tomato paste (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Onion, yellow (1 large, diced)
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Ginger, fresh (1-inch piece, grated) — pantry
- Chicken or vegetable broth (1 cup) — pantry
- Cumin (1 tsp), coriander (1 tsp), red pepper flakes or cayenne (to taste) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Vegetable oil — pantry
- Sweet potato (1 large, peeled and cubed) — optional, adds color and heartiness
- Fufu powder (prepared per package) OR jasmine rice — pantry
- Fresh parsley or cilantro (for garnish)
- Pineapple (½ fresh or 1 can chunks — served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pat goat pieces dry and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of cumin. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over high heat. Brown goat pieces in batches, 3–4 minutes per side — don't crowd the pot or they'll steam instead of sear. Remove and set aside.
2. Reduce heat to medium. In the same pot, sauté diced onion 3 minutes. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, cook 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste and let it cook until it darkens slightly, about 2 minutes.
3. Add canned tomatoes, broth, cumin, coriander, and cayenne. Return the browned goat to the pot. Scoop out a ladleful of the hot cooking liquid, whisk the peanut butter into it until smooth, then stir it back into the pot (this prevents the peanut butter from clumping). Add sweet potato if using.
4. Cover and simmer over medium-low heat for 30–35 minutes, stirring occasionally, until goat is tender and the stew has thickened to a rich sauce. Taste and adjust salt and heat.
5. **Fufu:** Prepare according to package instructions (typically: boil water, remove from heat or reduce, whisk in fufu powder vigorously until thick and smooth and it pulls away from the sides). Portion into balls.
6. Serve stew over fufu or rice. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro. Serve pineapple chunks on the side.
