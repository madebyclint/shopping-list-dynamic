## Deeper View

**Simple Maggi Ribs w/ Couscous & Roasted Broccoli** *(Sunday, May 18)*
Total time: ~40 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

> Maggi sauce — soy-forward, slightly sweet — caramelizes on beef short ribs into a deeply savory crust. Fluffy couscous soaks up every bit of the pan drippings, and roasted broccoli adds crunch and a slight char. Same fast Maggi magic, earthier and more filling sides.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef short ribs (bone-in, ~3 lbs)
- Maggi seasoning sauce (generous coat)
- Couscous (1½ cups dry)
- Broccoli (2 heads, cut into florets)
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper

**Cooking Overview**

1. Marinate ribs in Maggi sauce at least 30 min at room temp (or overnight in fridge).
2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss broccoli florets with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet.
3. Roast broccoli 20–22 min, flipping once, until edges are crispy and charred.
4. Heat cast iron or heavy skillet over medium-high. Sear ribs 3–4 min per side until deeply browned and Maggi caramelizes.
5. Transfer skillet to oven (or finish ribs on a rack) for 12–15 min until meat pulls slightly from bone.
6. While ribs finish, bring 1½ cups water to a boil with a pinch of salt. Pour over couscous, cover, rest 5 min, then fluff with a fork.
7. Plate ribs over a mound of couscous with roasted broccoli alongside.

**Steamed Artichokes & Al Pastor Wings** *(Wednesday, May 21)*
Total time: ~45 min · Approx calories: ~620 per adult

> Steamed whole artichokes are a slow, communal eat — pull leaves, drag through butter, work toward the heart. Paired with fast air fryer wings coated in al pastor seasoning and hit with arbol chili sauce, it's a fun, hands-on dinner that feels special without much effort.

**Ingredients Used**

- Whole artichokes (4 large)
- Lemon (1, halved — for steaming water)
- Butter (for dipping, melted)
- Chicken wings (3–4 lbs, split)
- Al pastor seasoning (generous coat)
- Arbol chili sauce (for serving)
- Zucchini or corn (1–2, simple roasted veggie side)
- Mango (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Trim artichoke stems flush and snip sharp leaf tips. Bring a large pot of salted water with lemon halves to a boil.
2. Steam artichokes stem-down, covered, 30–35 min until outer leaves pull off easily and base is tender when pierced.
3. While artichokes steam, pat wings dry and toss generously with al pastor seasoning.
4. Air fry wings at 400°F for 20–22 min, flipping halfway, until crispy and cooked through.
5. Melt butter and pour into small dipping bowls.
6. Roast or sauté zucchini/corn as a simple side while wings finish.
7. Serve artichokes with melted butter, wings with arbol chili sauce on the side, mango sliced fresh.

**Fried Chicken, Salad & Fruit** *(Thursday, May 22 — TEEN PREP)*
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~540 per adult

> Classic crispy fried chicken — well-seasoned, golden, crunchy — served alongside a simple mixed green salad and fresh fruit. Easy enough for teens to own, satisfying enough for everyone. Dredge, fry, done.

**Ingredients Used**

- Bone-in chicken pieces (~3 lbs, legs and/or thighs)
- Buttermilk (1 cup, for soaking)
- All-purpose flour (1 cup, for dredging)
- Garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne, salt, black pepper
- Neutral oil (for frying)
- Mixed greens or romaine (1 bag)
- Salad dressing (1 bottle)
- Fresh fruit (sliced melon, berries, or whatever is on hand)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Season chicken pieces with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Soak in buttermilk 15–30 min (or up to overnight).
2. Mix flour with garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper in a shallow dish.
3. Heat 1–2 inches of neutral oil in a heavy skillet or Dutch oven to 350°F.
4. Dredge chicken in seasoned flour, shaking off excess.
5. Fry in batches, turning once, until golden brown and cooked through — about 12–15 min for thighs/legs.
6. Drain on a rack or paper towels. Season immediately with a pinch of salt.
7. Toss greens with dressing. Plate chicken with salad and sliced fruit on the side.

**Pancit Bihon** *(Monday, May 19)*
Total time: ~50 min · Approx calories: ~540 per adult

> Classic Filipino stir-fried rice vermicelli — seared pork and chicken, slow-caramelized onions, crisp-tender carrots, celery, snap peas, and cabbage, all tossed in a savory soy-oyster sauce broth. One wok, one meal, completely satisfying.

**Ingredients Used**

- Boneless chicken thighs (~1 lb, cut into 1" cubes)
- Pork shoulder (~8 oz, cut into ½" cubes)
- Yellow onion (1 medium, sliced)
- Garlic (7–8 cloves, minced)
- Carrots (2 medium, julienned)
- Celery (2 ribs, thinly sliced on a bias)
- Sugar snap peas (1 cup, sliced on a bias)
- Green cabbage (4 cups, cut into 1" pieces)
- Rice vermicelli noodles / pancit bihon (8 oz)
- Oyster sauce (3 tbsp), soy sauce (3 tbsp), fish sauce (1–2 tsp)
- Chicken stock (4 cups)
- Green onions + lemon or lime (for garnish)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Mise en place first — slice all veg and cube meats before turning on the wok.
2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large wok over medium-high until lightly smoking. Season pork with salt & pepper, stir-fry 3–4 min until browned on all sides. Set aside.
3. Add 1 tbsp oil, add chicken, sear 2–3 min without touching, then stir-fry 3–4 min until cooked through. Set aside with pork.
4. Reduce heat to medium-low. In the rendered fat, sauté onion with a pinch of salt for 10–12 min until golden and soft. Add garlic, cook 30–45 sec. Set aside with meats.
5. Crank heat to high. Add last tbsp of oil, then stir-fry carrots, celery, snap peas, and cabbage for 90 seconds. Set aside with everything else.
6. In the wok over medium heat, add oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, and chicken stock. Bring to a simmer.
7. Add noodles; stir constantly for 3–4 min until they absorb the liquid and are tender. Add a splash more stock if too dry.
8. Return all meats and veg to the wok, toss to combine. Squeeze lime over the top, garnish with sliced green onions.
