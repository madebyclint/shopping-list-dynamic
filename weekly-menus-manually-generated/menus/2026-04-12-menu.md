**🥪 Simple Francesinha** *(SUNDAY — Special, April 12)*
Total time: ~40 min · Approx calories: ~700 per adult

> **Pork-free house version.** The traditional Porto Francesinha uses linguiça (pork sausage) and cured ham. We use deli turkey and all-beef hot dogs — same chaotic energy, no pork.
> Recipe spirit from the streets of Porto. For the classic original, see: [Portugalist — How to Make Francesinha](https://www.portugalist.com/francesinha-recipe/)

**What it is:** The messiest, most satisfying open-top grilled sandwich you'll eat this year. Layers of turkey and seared beef sausage on thick bread, smothered in melted Swiss cheese, then drowned in a hot tomato soup sauce. Fork required. Dignity optional.

**Ingredients Used**

- Thick white bread (8 slices — Texas toast or a thick bakery loaf)
- Deli turkey, sliced (~6 oz)
- Beef hot dogs or beef kielbasa (3–4 links, sliced lengthwise)
- Swiss cheese or provolone, sliced (6–8 slices — the more the better)
- Campbell's Healthy Request Tomato Soup (1 can, ~10.75 oz) — the sauce base
- Beer, lager (1 can, optional but good) — splash in while warming the soup
- Worcestershire sauce (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Cholula or hot sauce (to taste) — pantry
- Butter (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt, black pepper — pantry
- Frozen fries (1 bag) — oven, alongside

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Fries:** Preheat oven to 425°F. Spread fries on a baking sheet. Bake 20–25 min, flip halfway, until golden.
2. **Sausage:** Slice hot dogs or kielbasa lengthwise. Brown in a dry skillet over medium-high, 2–3 min per side until charred. Set aside.
3. **Sauce:** Pour tomato soup into a small saucepan. Add a splash of beer (2–3 oz), a dash of Worcestershire, and hot sauce to taste. Warm over medium-low, stirring, until hot. Don't cook it down too much — you want it pourable.
4. **Build sandwiches:** Butter one side of each bread slice. Lay butter-side-down in a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Stack: turkey → seared sausage → second slice of bread, butter-side-up. Pan-grill like a grilled cheese until both sides are golden, 3–4 min per side.
5. **Sauce and cheese:** Place sandwiches in an oven-safe dish or rimmed pan. Ladle hot sauce generously over each one — it should pool around and over them. Lay Swiss slices on top to cover completely. Broil 2–3 min until cheese is bubbly and starting to brown. Watch it.
6. Serve straight from the dish. Fries on the side. Offer extra sauce at the table.

> **Tip:** Muenster works just as well as Swiss if that's what's available. And yes — add more cheese on top if you're feeling feral. That's the move.

**🍲 Opor Ayam Putih (Chicken Braised in Coconut Milk & Spices)** *(MONDAY, April 13)*
Total time: ~50 min (Instant Pot) · Approx calories: ~550 per adult · Approx calories: ~550 per adult

> Recipe from *Mortar & Pestle* by Patricia Tanumihardja, p. 56. A white coconut milk braise — Julia says it's just like Thai green curry. *Oma says: sneak in Terasi Bakar to pump up the flavor.*

**Ingredients Used — Spice Paste**

- Macadamia nuts, unsalted (5) — or candlenuts
- Coriander seeds (1 tbsp) — or ground coriander; check pantry
- Asian shallots (8) — or 4 European shallots, chopped
- Garlic (4 cloves, smashed) — pantry
- Ground cumin (1 tsp) — pantry
- Ground white or black pepper (1 tsp) — pantry
- Vegetable oil (4 tbsp / 60 ml, divided) — pantry

**Ingredients Used — Braise**

- Whole chicken, 3–4 lbs (cut into 8 parts)
- Salam leaves or makrut lime leaves (3 leaves)
- Galangal, fresh (2 slices, ½-inch thick)
- Lemongrass (1 stalk, trimmed and bruised)
- Dried kencur, soaked (3 slices) — or ½ tsp ground kencur *(optional)*
- Water (2 cups / 475 ml)
- Coconut milk, unsweetened (2 cups / 475 ml, divided — ~2 cans)
- Fine sea salt (1½ tbsp)
- Sugar (1 tbsp)
- Fried shallots — for serving
- Jasmine rice — pantry

**Cooking Overview (Instant Pot)**

1. **Toast:** In a small dry skillet over medium heat, toast macadamias and coriander seeds separately until fragrant and lightly browned, 5–6 min. Crush nuts with the flat of a knife; grind coriander coarsely.
2. **Spice paste:** Pulse toasted nuts, coriander, shallots, garlic, cumin, and pepper in a food processor with 2 tbsp oil until the texture of rough oatmeal, about 1 min. Scrape down sides as needed.
3. **Sauté paste:** Set Instant Pot to **Sauté (Normal)**. Add remaining 2 tbsp oil. Once hot, add the spice paste and cook, stirring, until very fragrant and a shade darker, 5–7 min. Hit Cancel when paste is done.
4. **Load the pot:** Add chicken pieces, salam/makrut leaves, galangal, lemongrass, and kencur (if using). Stir to coat. Pour in the water and 1 cup (240 ml) coconut milk. Add salt and sugar. Stir to combine.
5. **Pressure cook:** Seal the lid and set valve to Sealing. Cook on **High Pressure for 15 minutes**. When done, let pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully quick-release the rest.
6. **Finish:** Open lid. Switch back to Sauté mode. Stir in the remaining 1 cup coconut milk and simmer 2–3 min, stirring. Taste and adjust salt. Let rest 10–15 min if time allows — it gets better.
7. **Serve:** Ladle over jasmine rice. Top generously with fried shallots.

**🍖 Simple Maggi Beef Ribs** *(TUESDAY, April 14)*
Total time: ~45 min (mostly passive) · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

> All-beef ribs — Julian's preference. This is as simple as it gets: Maggi sauce is the entire recipe.

**Ingredients Used**

- Beef short ribs, all-beef (2–3 lbs / multiple packs) — Ideal Foods
- Maggi seasoning sauce (generous pour, ~4–5 tbsp) — pantry
- Kale (1 bunch, leaves torn, stems removed)
- Olive oil (1–2 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt — pantry
- Pineapple (fresh, sliced or cubed — as a palate cleanser side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Marinate ribs:** Place ribs in a bowl or zip bag. Pour Maggi sauce over them — coat generously on all sides. Marinate at least 30 min at room temp, or overnight in the fridge if you can plan ahead.
2. **Cook ribs:** Heat a cast iron or heavy skillet over medium-high. Sear ribs 3–4 min per side until deeply browned and the Maggi caramelizes on the surface. Lower heat and cook through, another 5–8 min, or finish in a 375°F oven for 12–15 min. All-beef short ribs are done when the meat pulls slightly from the bone.
3. **Kale chips:** While ribs cook, preheat oven to 375°F. Spread torn kale leaves in a single layer on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, toss to coat, season with a pinch of salt. Roast 10–12 min until crispy at the edges but not burnt. They go fast — check at 10 min.
4. **Plate:** Ribs + kale chips + fresh pineapple slices. Done.

**🇨🇷 Costa Rica Casado with Salsa Lizano** *(WEDNESDAY, April 15)*
Total time: ~35 min · Approx calories: ~550 per adult

> *Casado* means "married man's plate" — the classic Costa Rican lunch/dinner. Rice + black beans + fried sweet plantains + salad + protein, all on one plate. Salsa Lizano is the key: a mild, slightly smoky condiment that goes on everything.

**Ingredients Used**

- Chicken thighs, boneless skinless (~1 lb) — Ideal Foods
- Garlic (3 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Olive oil (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Salt, pepper, cumin — pantry
- Salsa Lizano (2–3 tbsp for cooking + bottle on the table)
- Jasmine rice (1½ cups dry) — pantry
- Black beans, canned (2 cans, ~15 oz each)
- Plantains, ripe/maduros (2–3, sliced on the diagonal)
- Vegetable oil (for pan-frying plantains) — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Rice first:** Start jasmine rice — 18 min covered. Everything else fits in that window.
2. **Black beans:** Drain and rinse. Warm in a small saucepan with a pinch of cumin, a pinch of salt, and 2 tbsp Salsa Lizano. Simmer 5 min on low. Keep warm.
3. **Fried plantains (platanos maduros):** Slice plantains diagonally, ~½ inch thick. Heat ¼ inch of vegetable oil in a skillet over medium. Fry 1½–2 min per side until golden and caramelized. Drain on paper towels, lightly salt.
4. **Chicken:** Season thighs with salt, pepper, and cumin. Sear in olive oil over medium-high, 5–6 min per side until cooked through and golden. Drizzle 1–2 tbsp Salsa Lizano in the last 2 min — let it caramelize on the chicken.
5. **Plate:** Rice base → black beans → fried plantains → sliced chicken. Salsa Lizano bottle on the table.

**🍱 Japanese Teriyaki Chicken Bowls** *(THURSDAY TEEN PREP — April 16)*
Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~510 per adult

> Teen-prep level: easy. Julian can run this start to finish. The glaze is sweet enough to eat with a spoon. Last made week of Feb 19.

**Ingredients Used**

- Chicken thighs, boneless skinless (~2 lbs) — Ideal Foods
- Broccoli (1 small head or bag of florets) — produce
- Jasmine rice (1½ cups dry) — pantry
- Soy sauce (¼ cup) — pantry
- Honey (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Garlic (2 cloves, minced) — pantry
- Sesame oil (1 tsp) — pantry
- Rice vinegar or mirin (1 tbsp) — pantry
- Cornstarch (1 tsp + 1 tbsp cold water, optional — thickens the glaze) — pantry
- Scallions (garnish) — already in produce
- Sesame seeds (garnish, optional) — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Sauce:** Whisk soy sauce, honey, garlic, sesame oil, and rice vinegar in a small bowl. If you want a thicker glaze, mix cornstarch with 1 tbsp cold water and stir it in.
2. **Rice:** Start jasmine rice (18 min covered) while you prep everything else.
3. **Broccoli:** Steam or microwave florets until just tender, 4–5 min. Or toss with olive oil and a pinch of salt and roast at 400°F for 15 min alongside the chicken.
4. **Chicken:** Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high. Cook thighs 5–6 min per side until just cooked through. Pour the teriyaki sauce over, reduce heat to medium, and cook 2–3 more min until the glaze thickens and coats. Rest 2 min, then slice.
5. **Plate:** Rice → broccoli → sliced chicken → drizzle any remaining glaze → scallions + optional sesame seeds.

> **Teen tip:** Set a timer when the rice goes in. Everything else finishes in the same window. Watch the glaze at the end — it goes from caramelized to burnt fast on high heat. Medium is your friend.
