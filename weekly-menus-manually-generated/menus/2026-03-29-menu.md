**🥩 Seared Steak with Pearl Couscous & Roasted Broccoli** *(SUNDAY — Birthday, March 29)*
Total time: ~35–40 min · Approx calories: ~700 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- NY strip or ribeye steaks (~2 lbs total, 4 steaks) — Brooklyn Fare recommended for quality
- Pearl couscous (1 box, ~8 oz)
- Chicken or vegetable broth (1½ cups) — pantry
- Butter (2 tbsp) — pantry
- Garlic (2–3 cloves) — pantry
- Fresh parsley or thyme (optional garnish)
- Broccoli (1 large head, cut into florets)
- Olive oil, salt, black pepper, red pepper flakes — pantry
- Lemon (1, for finishing) — pantry or pick up

**Cooking Overview**

1. **Broccoli:** Toss florets with olive oil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Roast at 425°F for 20–22 min until charred at edges.
2. **Pearl couscous:** Toast dry couscous in 1 tbsp butter in a small saucepan over medium heat for 2–3 min. Add garlic, then broth. Simmer covered 10–12 min until tender. Fluff with fork, finish with parsley and a squeeze of lemon.
3. **Steaks:** Pat dry, season generously with salt and pepper. Sear in a very hot cast iron or heavy pan with 1 tbsp oil for 3–4 min per side (medium-rare to medium). Rest 5 min before serving.
4. Plate steak over or alongside couscous. Broccoli on the side. Optional: pan drippings as a quick pan sauce.

**🍜 Meatball Ramen with Frozen Mixed Veg** *(TUESDAY FAST — March 31)*
Total time: ~20 min · Approx calories: ~520 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Ramen noodle packs (4 individual packs — use noodles + broth seasoning)
- Frozen beef meatballs (16–20 ct, from bag) — ~5 per person
- Frozen mixed vegetables (1 bag, ~12 oz)
- Scallions (2–3, sliced for garnish)
- Soy sauce (1–2 tbsp drizzle) — pantry
- Sesame oil (optional drizzle) — pantry
- Soft-boiled eggs (optional add-on) — pantry

**Cooking Overview**

1. Heat broth in a pot: 4 cups water + ramen seasoning packets (use 1–2 full + taste). Bring to simmer.
2. Add frozen meatballs and mixed veg to broth. Simmer 6–8 min until meatballs are heated through.
3. Cook ramen noodles in a separate pot (2–3 min), or add directly to broth for final 3 min.
4. Ladle into bowls. Top with sliced scallions, a drizzle of soy sauce or sesame oil. Add a soft-boiled egg if desired.

**🍝 Spaghetti & Impossible Meatballs** *(WEDNESDAY — Friends Arrive, April 1 — Vegetarian, serves 7)*
> Fried rice removed from this night.
Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

**Ingredients Used**

- Spaghetti pasta (2 lbs / 2 boxes) — 2 boxes ensures plenty for 7
- Marinara sauce, jarred (2 jars, ~24 oz each) — Rao's or store brand
- Impossible Meatballs, frozen (2 packs) — cook per package directions
- Parmesan, grated (optional) — pantry or pick up small block
- Fresh basil or parsley (optional) — pantry
- Salt, olive oil — pantry
- Oranges (5–7, served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti per package (10–11 min for al dente). Drain, toss with a bit of olive oil.
2. **Meatballs:** Cook Impossible meatballs in a large skillet per package — either bake at 400°F for 18–20 min or pan-sear in olive oil over medium-high, 8–10 min, turning until browned. Add marinara sauce to the skillet and simmer together 5 min.
3. Serve pasta topped with sauce and meatballs. Optional grated parmesan. Oranges on the side.
