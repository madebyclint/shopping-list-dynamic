## Deeper View

**Pepes Tahu / Banana Leaf-Wrapped Tofu Packets** *(Monday, May 4)*

Total time: ~55 min · Approx calories: ~380 per adult

> Pepes Tahu is a classic Indonesian dish from the Sundanese tradition — spiced, crumbled tofu mixed with egg and aromatics, wrapped tightly in banana leaves and steamed then grilled or broiled for charred edges. The banana leaf infuses the tofu with a subtle grassy sweetness that no other method replicates. Recipe from *Mortar & Pestle* (Let's Grill, p. 134–135). Makes 10 packets — serves 4 with rice and a vegetable side.

**Ingredients Used**

- 1 lb (450g) medium-firm tofu
- 1 tsp coriander seeds (or ground coriander)
- 8 Asian shallots or 4 European shallots, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 2–4 medium-hot finger-length red chilies (Fresno, cayenne, or serrano), seeded and chopped
- 3–4 tsp sambal oelek
- 1½ tsp fine sea salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- ½ tsp ground white or black pepper
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
- 2 makrut lime leaves
- 1 stalk lemongrass, trimmed and bruised
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- ½ cup (15g) tightly packed lemon basil, cilantro, or mint leaves, plus more for garnish
- 2 large eggs
- Banana leaves cut into 10 (9-inch/23-cm) squares
- Toothpicks or a stapler (for sealing packets)
- Mango slices (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Wrap tofu in a non-terry kitchen cloth and wring out most of the liquid. You should have about 2 cups (450g) crumbled tofu. Place in a large bowl.
2. Toast coriander seeds in a small dry skillet over medium heat until fragrant and browned, 5–6 minutes. Grind in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. (Skip if using ground coriander.)
3. In a food processor or blender, pulse ground coriander, shallots, garlic, chilies, salt, sugar, and white pepper with 1 tablespoon of oil until the texture of oatmeal, about 1 minute. Scrape down sides as needed.
4. Heat remaining 2 tablespoons of oil in a large heavy skillet over medium heat until shimmering. Add the spice paste, lime leaves, and lemongrass. Cook, stirring, until very fragrant and a few shades darker, 5–7 minutes. Reduce heat if browning too fast. Once moisture has evaporated, ingredients will separate from the oil. Cool 5 minutes, then remove lime leaves and lemongrass.
5. Add spice paste, bell pepper, and green onions to the crumbled tofu. Finely chop lemon basil and add immediately. Taste and adjust seasonings. Add eggs and mix until well incorporated.
6. Lay a banana leaf dull-side up with ribs parallel to you. Scoop 3 tablespoons of tofu mixture into the center. Top with 1–2 whole makrut lime leaves if desired. Fold square corners in toward each other to form a triangle. Trim sides with scissors. Secure ends with a toothpick or stapler.
7. Arrange packets on a steamer rack. Steam on medium heat 15–20 minutes. Carefully lift lid away from you. Open a packet to check — tofu should be firm. Steam a few more minutes if needed.
8. Grill or broil packets on high heat about 5 minutes until outer leaves are charred in spots.
9. Serve warm with steamed jasmine rice and a vegetable side dish. Mango slices on the side.

**Sushi Hand Rolls (Temaki)** *(Tuesday FAST, May 5)*

Total time: ~15 min · Approx calories: ~420 per adult

> Temaki (hand rolls) are the most forgiving form of sushi — no rolling mat, no precise technique, just nori wrapped around seasoned rice and fillings into a cone. Imitation crab keeps this budget-friendly and kid-approved. Using homemade mayo from earlier in the week makes the sriracha mayo feel extra special. A total no-stress DIY dinner for a late Tuesday night.

**Ingredients Used**

- 2 packs microwave sushi rice (or 2-minute jasmine rice packets)
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 pack imitation crab sticks, shredded or pulled apart
- 1 avocado, sliced
- ½ cucumber, julienned
- 8–10 nori sheets, halved
- 3 tbsp homemade mayo (or store-bought)
- 1–2 tsp sriracha
- Soy sauce (for dipping)
- Mandarin oranges (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Microwave rice packets per instructions. While warm, stir in rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Spread on a plate or baking sheet to cool for 5 minutes.
2. Slice avocado and julienne cucumber. Pull imitation crab apart into strands.
3. Mix mayo and sriracha together in a small bowl to make sriracha mayo.
4. Set out all fillings, nori halves, rice, soy sauce, and sriracha mayo buffet-style on the table.
5. Each person takes a half-sheet of nori, places a small scoop of rice on the left side, adds fillings, and rolls into a cone shape. Eat immediately.

**English/Scottish Breakfast for Dinner** *(Wednesday, May 6)*

Total time: ~30 min · Approx calories: ~540 per adult

> The full British fry-up reimagined as a weeknight dinner — a tradition known as "brinner." Roasted tomatoes, golden mushrooms, soft eggs, sausage, baked beans, and toast come together on one plate without any complicated technique. Comforting, protein-rich, and endlessly customizable around the table's pork preferences.

**Ingredients Used**

- 8 eggs
- 4–6 turkey sausage links (non-pork members) or pork sausage links (pork members, cooked separately)
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes
- 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 1 can baked beans (low-sodium preferred)
- 4–6 slices bread
- Salt and pepper
- Sliced oranges (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss cherry tomatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a small baking sheet. Roast 15 minutes.
2. Cook sausage links in a skillet over medium heat, turning occasionally, until cooked through, about 10 minutes. If serving both pork and turkey sausage, use separate pans.
3. In the same skillet, add a knob of butter and sauté mushrooms with a pinch of salt over medium-high heat until golden, about 5 minutes.
4. Warm baked beans in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally.
5. Fry or scramble eggs to each person's preference.
6. Toast bread. Arrange everything on plates family-style and serve immediately.

**Pesto Pasta with Chicken Sausage & Cherry Tomatoes** *(Thursday TEEN PREP, May 7)*

Total time: ~25 min · Approx calories: ~580 per adult

> A classic Italian-American weeknight pasta that teenagers can fully own. Store-bought pesto does the heavy lifting, pre-cooked sausage means no raw meat handling, and burst cherry tomatoes add a fresh brightness that makes the whole dish feel homemade. Three steps, one pan, done.

**Ingredients Used**

- 12 oz penne or fusilli pasta
- 2 pre-cooked chicken sausage links, sliced into rounds
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes
- ½ cup store-bought basil pesto
- ¼ cup parmesan, grated
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- Sliced strawberries (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, scoop out ¼ cup of pasta water. Drain pasta.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add sliced chicken sausage and cook until lightly browned, about 4 minutes. Add cherry tomatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, until they burst and release their juices, about 3 minutes.
3. Add drained pasta to the skillet. Stir in pesto and toss to coat. Add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce if needed. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Serve topped with grated parmesan and sliced strawberries on the side.

**Maggi-Marinated Ribs + Kimchi Slaw** *(Friday/Weekend, May 8–9)*

Total time: ~45 min active (plus 30 min+ marinade) · Approx calories: ~680 per adult

> Maggi seasoning sauce — a pantry staple across Southeast Asia, Latin America, and West Africa — caramelizes beautifully in the air fryer, giving ribs a deeply savory umami crust without a complicated marinade. Paired with a quick gochugaru-spiked napa cabbage slaw, this is a Korean-American BBQ fusion that comes together almost entirely hands-off.

**Ingredients Used**

- 2–3 lbs pork spare ribs or St. Louis-style ribs (pork portion)
- 1–1.5 lbs beef short ribs (non-pork portion)
- 3–4 tbsp Maggi seasoning sauce
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 2 cups napa cabbage, shredded
- 1 medium carrot, shredded
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp gochugaru (or red pepper flakes)
- 2 cups jasmine rice
- Navel oranges (served on the side)

**Cooking Overview**

1. Pat ribs dry with paper towels. Rub all over with Maggi sauce, garlic powder, and black pepper. Marinate at least 30 minutes at room temperature, or overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
2. Preheat air fryer to 375°F. Cook pork ribs for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until caramelized and cooked through. Cook beef short ribs in a separate batch using the same method and timing.
3. While ribs cook, toss shredded napa cabbage and carrot with rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, and gochugaru in a bowl. Let sit at least 10 minutes for the slaw to soften and meld.
4. Cook jasmine rice per package instructions.
5. Serve ribs over or alongside rice with kimchi slaw. Navel oranges on the side.
